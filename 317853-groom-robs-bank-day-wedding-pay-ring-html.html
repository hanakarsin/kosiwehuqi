Groom robs bank day before wedding to pay for ring

- The man was set to wed the following day but had not paid for the venue and ring

- Heath Bumpous walked into a bank and demanded for money from cashiers

- The groom was captured in CCTV cameras and the police shared the photos on social media

- Heath's fiancee noticed her husband-to-be was on police radar and asked him to surrender to police

A bridegroom in Texas, United States, has robbed a bank to buy a wedding ring and pay for venue, barely a day to his wedding.

In a report by Trinity county police boss Woody Wallace, Heath Bumpous robbed the Citizens State Bank in nearby Groveton on Friday, October 4.

In a report by CNBC, the 36-year-old drove into the bank's parking lot fully armed and demanded money from a cashier.

He left the scene of crime and drove off with the cash after his fiancee called him.

When the fiancee saw surveillance video stills of her husband-to-be robbing the bank that police had posted on Facebook, she persuaded him to surrender.

He turned himself in and confessed to the authorities that he had stolen the money to help in his wedding preparations that was set to happen the following day.

“He basically stated he was getting married tomorrow, he didn't have enough money for the wedding ring he wanted to buy and he needed to pay for the wedding venue,” Wallace said.

Police recovered most of the stolen money and the suspect is in currently in jail. The wedding was cancelled.

"No wedding took place that I know of...it didn't happen in my jail," said Wallace.
