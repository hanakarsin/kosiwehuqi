Eugene Wamalwa, Ojaamong' Call on Mudavadi, Wetang'ula to Support Raila in His Presidential Bid

  • According to Eugene Wamalwa by choosing to stand with Raila Odinga, the western region will have a greater chance of being in the next government
  • Wamalwa said the western region has to make a decision of backing a presidential candidate that has higher chances of winning the 2022 presidential race
  • On his part, Ojaamong urged Mudavadi and Wetang'ula to rally behind Raila because of his age, alluding that may only reign for one term if he clinches presidency

Devolution Cabinet Secretary Eugene Wamalwa and Busia governor Sospeter Ojaamong' have called on One Kenya Alliance (OKA) pair from the western region to once again support ODM party leader Raila Odinga in his bid to become president in 2022.

The leaders urged Musalia Mudavadi of the ANC party and Moses Wetang'ula of Ford Kenya to drop their grandstanding and back Raila in his 2022 presidential quest.

Wamalwa and Ojaamong' warned that the duo's hardline positions could deny the region strategic position at the decision-making table positions in the next government alluding that their presidential interests were not viable.

"I am looking for my brother Musalia and Wetang'ula because if we delay we will find ourselves outside the next government," Wamalwa said.

Wamalwa further stated that the region must make a firm decision ahead of the 2022 General Election or risk staying in the political cold for another five or ten years.

"We must make a decision that will land us the western region inside the next government and we have to do it now," he added.

Supporting Raila good for Musalia, Weta's political bids

On his part, Ojaamong' claimed the whole country was rooting for Raila as the best candidate to take Kenya to the next level, thus the need by all Kenyans to support his bid.

Ojaamong' also urged Mudavadi and Wetang'ula to throw their weight behind Raila because of his age, adding that he will only serve for five years (one term), thus a vantage point where the duo (Wetang'ula and Mudavadi) can advance their presidential dreams.

"We want Wetang'ula and Mudavadi to be alert and rally behind Raila since he is only going for five years, then he will leave this seat for us," he said.

OKA will not support Raila

The duo's sentiments came after the KANU secretary-general Nick Salat said OKA partners cannot ignore the Raila in its game plan ahead of the 2022 elections.

According to Salat, Raila is very important in their coalition, even as they plan to outnumber Deputy President William Ruto in the coming general election.

“Everybody is insisting on their preferred candidate and how they are suitable to be the flag bearer, however, it is important for people to sit down and talk. It is about what formula will hand us a win, not the question of who has what numbers,” Salat said.
