Mourners captured on video vigorously dancing to Wamlambez hit song at funeral

In Kenya, viral phrases and new waves often become old news as soon as a new song is discovered or an exciting word is coined.

However, the phrase Wamlambez Wamnyonyez has refused to die or disappear in peace.

Netizens were treated to a rare sight when a group of revelers took their ratchetness to a funeral.

While many would expect burials to be marked with incessant sobs and tight embraces, a new generation of mourners decided to pay their respects with provocative dancing.

READ ALSO: TV sensation Doreen Majala buys KSh 3 million Mercedez Benz, thanks her maker

And the song they were raging to was the infamous Wamlambez by boy band Sailors.

Like any other funeral, the traditional black themed attire was observed and there were bouquets of flowers placed on the graveside.

Children were also present and the dust had not even settled before mourners broke into wild dancing.

Even the young ones joined in as the mass chanted ''mh ah na mayengs" in unison.

Then like a group of coached dancers, the mourners showed off their dancing skills and let their bodies do the talking.

In the video shared all over Facebook, at some point, mourners went around the casket as they shook their money makers like money hungry thrill seekers.

Of course Kenyans just watched in shock and wondered how corrupted the world has become to a point people have forgotten to respect the dead.
