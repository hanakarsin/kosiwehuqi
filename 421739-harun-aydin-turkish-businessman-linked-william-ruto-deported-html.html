Harun Aydin: Turkish Businessman Linked to William Ruto Deported

  • Harun Aydin was at the centre of the saga surrounding William Ruto's aborted trip to Uganda
  • Aydin was reportedly bundled onto a Turkish airline en route to Istanbul at about 5am
  • The Turk's lead lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi vowed to challenge his deportation in court

Turkish businessman Harun Aydin linked to Deputy President William Ruto has been deported.

Aydin, who was at the centre of the saga surrounding Ruto's aborted trip to Uganda, was deported on the morning of Monday, August 9, and handed to Turkish authorities.

Confirming the incident, the Turk's lead lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi said they were ready to represent him at Kahawa Law Courts when they were informed of the new development.

Consequently, he vowed to challenge the decision in court insisting that the deportation was done without following the required procedure.

Further, the Grand Mullah lamented that for the two days their client was in custody, since his arrest on Saturday, August 7, they were not allowed to see him.

The businessman was reportedly bundled onto a Turkish airline at about 5am and landed in Istanbul where he was picked by the country's security officials for further grilling.

Aydin was one of the individuals who were blocked to fly to Uganda from Kenya with Ruto on Monday, August 2.

He, however, flew to Uganda alongside politicians Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro, Kapseret counterpart Oscar Sudi, Kinango MP Benjamin Tayari, Elijah Rono, Eric Ruto and David Langat.

The immigration officials at the Wilson Airport later picked him for questioning after landing from Uganda.

Taking to his social media platforms, Ruto said Aydin had been dragged into a tragedy of political pettiness since he had valid work permits yet he had been labelled a terrorist due to his association with the DP.

"Turkish investor, Aydin Harun, is a victim of top-down arrogance bred by patronage and cartels that criminalise enterprise," said Ruto.

An investor

Following the Wilson Airport drama that led to Ruto's trip being aborted, the Turkish Embassy in Nairobi came to the defence of Aydin.

The embassy said Aydin is an investor who has visited Kenya several times in the past using his Turkish passport.

The embassy further claimed the 54-year-old was just an investor who came to Kenya purposely for business work, and he is not a wanted person as many claimed.
