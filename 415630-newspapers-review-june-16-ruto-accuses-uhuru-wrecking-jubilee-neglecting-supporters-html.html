Newspapers Review for June 16: Ruto Accuses Uhuru of Wrecking Jubilee, Neglecting Supporters

On Wednesday, June 16, the national dailies widely reported about Deputy President William Ruto's reactions to reports that President Uhuru Kenyatta will back a NASA candidate in the 2022 General Election.

The papers also reported about the Form One placement of the 2020 Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) candidates.

1. Daily Nation

According to this publication, Deputy President William Ruto has accused his boss, President Uhuru Kenyatta of wrecking the Jubilee Party.

Ruto took to his social media platforms to react to reports that the head of state would support a presidential candidate from the National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition.

The deputy president accused Uhuru of abandoning Jubilee supporters, adding he is ready to go for the presidency even without the president's support.

Uhuru had earlier taken an indirect jibe at his deputy and said he has achieved more in his second term while working with the Opposition than he did in his first term.

2. Taifa Leo

The family of the late Fatuma Mwero whose body went missing from her grave has cried foul after they were prohibited from re-burying her remains, Taifa Leo reports.

Mwero's grave was found empty with the location of her remains being unknown. Over the weekend, the casket carrying her body was found 600 meters from her burial site.

The family said police officers had stopped them from burying their loved one again because of a land dispute. They, however, insisted the land on which Mwero had been buried belongs to them.

Before finding her body, the family had said they would bury a banana trunk in Mwero's empty grave as efforts to locate her body had not borne fruits.

3. The Standard

According to the Standard, businessman Jimmy Wanjigi has strongly hinted that he will join the race to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta in the 2022 General Election.

Wanjigi, who is credited with the successful merger of URP and TNA that gave birth to Uhuru and William Ruto's Jubilee, said he will no longer be a kingmaker but will instead go for the kill.

The tycoon said he will divulge more details about his ambitions in due time.

If he throws his hat in the ring, Wanjigi will join Ruto, Musalia Mudavadi, Mukhisa Kituyi, Kalonzo Musyoka all of who have expressed interest in the race to state house.

4. The Star

According to the Star, Treasury Cabinet Secretary Ukur Yatani has promised to release KSh 39 billion to counties by Friday, June 18, to avert shutdown as he lifted the lid on the financial crisis in the government.

Yatani pledged the disbursement when he appeared before a Senate committee on Tuesday, June 15, to explain the delayed release.

Yatani spoke on the financial crisis cutting across the government in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic which he said is taking a heavy toll on revenue performance.

5. People Daily

Four governors from the counties bordering the Lake Victoria Basin have asked the national government to impose a partial lockdown in select areas to curb the surge of infections.

Governors Anyang' Nyong'o (Kisumu), Sospeter Ojaamong (Busia), Wycliffe Wangamati (Bungoma), Wycliffe Oparanya (Kakamega), and Stephen Sang (Nandi) held a meeting with a team from the government where they made the suggestion.

They said a partial lockdown would stem the unfolding disaster which, if not controlled will turn the region into a hot spot.

COVID-19 cases in the Nyanza region started spiking after the Madaraka Day celebration which was held in Kisumu.
