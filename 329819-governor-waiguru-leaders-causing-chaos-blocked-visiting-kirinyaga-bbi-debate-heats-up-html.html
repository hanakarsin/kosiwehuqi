Governor Waiguru wants leaders causing chaos blocked from visiting Kirinyaga as BBI debate heats up

- Waiguru said she will not allow politicians from outside to divide Kirinyaga people along tribal and political lines

- The governor asked the county commissioner to ensure such leaders are locked out of the county

- The county boss has been reading from different pages with a section of Mt Kenya leaders particularly Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria

- The two have publicly differed over the manner in which the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) report should be implemented

Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru is seeking to have leaders known for causing chaos blocked from visiting and holding rallies in the county.

The governor who has been at odds with a section of Mt Kenya leaders allied to Tanga Tanga faction of the ruling Jubilee Party said she will not allow war-mongers to divide Kirinyaga residents along tribal and political lines.

Speaking during Jamhuri Day celebrations at Kiamutugu Secondary School on Thursday, December 12, the county leader asked the county commissioner to ensure such leaders are barred from holding rallies or meetings in the county.

"They have started bringing problems in Kirinyaga and we will not allow it. I want to ask the county commissioner not to allow that to happen," she said in reference to her political nemeses.

While the Kirinyaga leader did not mention names of leaders she wanted blocked, TUKO.co.ke understands the governor has been exchanging public tirades with Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria who has also turned out to be President Uhuru Kenyatta's fiercest critic.

The disagreement between the two leaders emanated from the simmering debate on whether the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) report should be implemented through Parliament or by way of referendum.

While Waiguru is for a referendum process which is supported by Opposition leader Raila Odinga, Kuria is leading the pro-parliamentary brigade and has been bombarding the pro-referendum team with accusations of extravagance.

The vocal Jubilee lawmaker was recently quoted telling off those warning him against disrespecting the president saying most of them were puppets and were only trying to create a rift between him and Uhuru in order to gain relevance.

He mentioned Waiguru, ex Kiambu governor William Kabogo and Trade and Industrialisation CS Peter Munya among leaders who had warned and even threatened to bar him from traversing some parts of Mt Kenya region.

The lawmaker proceeded to write a humiliating and most demeaning post on his Facebook page that portrayed the Kirinyaga governor as a corrupt and unreliable leader who terribly failed Jubilee government while serving as Devolution Cabinet Secretary.

The move by Waiguru to bar leaders who are marked for causing chaos further strained their relationship and the vocal MP did not miss the opportunity to reply:

"Her Royal Highness has declared Kirinyaga her bedroom and instructed the county commissioner not to allow other leaders there. Even Moi's dictatorship never reached this level".
