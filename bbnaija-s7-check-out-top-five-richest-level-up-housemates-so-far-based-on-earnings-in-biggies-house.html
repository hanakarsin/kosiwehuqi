Check Out Top Five Richest 'Level Up' Housemates So Far Based On Earnings In Biggie's House

The 7th edition of Africa’s biggest reality TV show, Big Brother Naija, is gradually winding down and one of the ‘Level Up’ Housemates will go home with the N100 million grand prize.

Apart from offering over 24 youths the opportunity to come to the spotlight in the entertainment industry, the housemates also had the chance to scoop huge sums of money into their bank accounts, through the interesting tasks and games on the show.

In this article, Naija News highlights top housemates who have stayed consistent with their wins in the course of the nine weeks so far.

Chomzy BBNaija

1. Chomzy: The reality TV star is currently topping as the richest ‘Level Up’ housemate. She has amassed about N8 million in cash prizes and also won a trip to Cape Town with the rest of her fellow housemates.

Some of her wins include N750,000 from Lush Hair, N250,000 from Close Up, N500,000 from Guinness Smooth, N2.5 million from Supa Komando, N220,000 from Airtel and N2.5 million from Pepsi, N2 million from WAW and many others.

Bryann

2. Bryann: He has gained about N4.8 million in his eight weeks stay in the Biggie house. Some of his wins include N250,000 from Close Up, N500,000 from Guinness Smooth, N2.5 million from Pepsi as well as an all-expense paid trip to South Africa.

Adekunle

3. Adekunle: He has so far won about N4.45 million. Some of the wins include N1.5 million from Piggyvest, N250,000 from Close Up, N2.5 million from Supa Komando, ₦1,760,000 from Quidax, and Trip to Cape Town from Pepsi.

Dotun

4. Dotun: He has won about N3.65 million in prizes so far some of which include N500,000 from Guinness Smooth, N150, 000 from Lush Hair, N1 million from TravelBeta, N200, 000 from headline sponsored, Pocket, ₦1,760,000 from Quidax, an all-expense paid trip to South Africa courtesy of Pepsi and many others.

Deji

5. Deji: The fake housemate made great use of his time on the BBNaija show before his sudden eviction. He made about N3.4 million before his exit. His wins include N500,000 from TravelBeta, N220,000 from Airtel, ₦1,760,000 from Quidax, N2.5 million from Pepsi and a trip to South Africa.
