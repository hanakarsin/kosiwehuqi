Kenyan businessman abducted in Tanzania found in Mombasa

- Raphael Ongangi was waylaid by three gunmen at the intersection of Msasani and Karume Road on Monday, June 24.

- The businessman was in company of his wife, Veronica Kundya, during the abduction

- Veronica who was let off the hook by abductors raised alarm through her Facebook page

- She also reported the incident to security officers in Tanzania who commenced instant investigations

Kenyan businessman who was abducted by unknown people in Tanzania has been found alive in Mombasa.

As was earlier reported, Raphael Ongangi was waylaid on Monday, June 24, at the intersection of Msasani and Karume Road by three gunmen who had cars and two motorbikes.

His wife, Veronica Kundya, who had accompanied Ongangi during the abduction confirmed on Tuesday, July 2, that his husband had been found in Mombasa, and was in good condition.

" I have talked to him and I can confirm he is in Mombasa and in good condition. I thank God for this and all those who helped in searching for him," said Veronica

She, however, did not disclose the exact place her husband was found and whether the police had arrested any suspect in connection with the incident.

" I don't have much to say because we have just talked briefly. The only thing I can confirm is that my husband is alive and well," she added

Veronica evoked emotions on her Facebook page after sharing a touching message following the abduction of her husband.

She asked the general public to help her trace the love of her life who she described as a loving and caring man.

"Children are asking for you every minute, Lindi is in hospital for the third day now. I thank God for my courage and faith that I have believing our God is great. I ask you to help me spread this message," she wrote

The post got attention of netizens, media outlets and security teams both in Kenya and Tanzania who launched investigation into the matter.

"We are aware of the incident and the mission is seized of the matter. The mission is working around the clock and will be sharing progress reports on this issue," said Kenyan High Commissioner to Tanzania Dan Kazungu

Ongangi has been running a transport business between Dar es Salam, DRC Congo, Zambia and Rwanda
