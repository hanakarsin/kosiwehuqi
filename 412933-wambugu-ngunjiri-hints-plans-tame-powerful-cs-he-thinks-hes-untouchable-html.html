Wambugu Ngunjiri Hints at Plans to Tame Powerful CS: &quot;He Thinks He's Untouchable&quot;

- Ngunjiri Wambugu accused the unnamed Cabinet secretary of harassing and running over politicians

- He vowed to mobilise resources and political supporters even from opponents to deal with the CS, whom he described as rogue

- ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna and MP Sudi recently lashed out at CS Matiang'i over the recent chaos witnessed during the Bonchari by-election

Outspoken Nyeri Town lawmaker Ngunjiri Wambugu has put an unnamed powerful Cabinet secretary on notice for his alleged overbearing and dictatorial tendencies.

The Jubilee MP wondered why the Cabinet secretary, a mere appointee of President Uhuru Kenyatta, often uses state machinery to harass politicians, including those who defend the government against critics.

"There is this one Cabinet secretary who somehow thinks he’s untouchable. We get insulted here every day as we protect the government he works for, then he thinks he can run over us the way he runs over everyone else," he alleged on Friday, May 21.

Wambugu vowed to mobilise resources and political supporters even from opponents to ensure the CS, whom he described as a rogue, was tamed and removed from his office no matter what.

"He will learn that he can’t, even if we have to pool resources with UDA and ODM and make dealing with him a joint project, since he’s already also tried to run over them too. Sometimes you must deal with the crack before it brings down the building. We can’t keep allowing some bureaucrats to keep making our politics harder than it already is, or should be," he warned.

Although Wambugu did not mention the CS' he was referring to, TUKO.co.ke understands his comments may be targeted the Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i.

Two days ago, ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna lashed out at Matiang'i over the recent chaos that erupted during the May 18 Bonchari by-election.

Taking to his Twitter, the SG blamed the super CS of "clobbering his own people in Kisii like they are animals", adding that he is a "beast".

The tough-talking ODM politician demanded that Uhuru sacks Matiangi's over his alleged involvement in the violence witnessed in the mini-poll.

"Matiangi behaves like a beast, clobbering his own people in Kisii like they are animals. Uhuru must sack Matiang'i or send him to the Ministry of Wildlife. He will be at home," he said.

It even became more apparent "the Super CS" was a marked man by politicians drawn from across the divide when Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi hinted at plans to impeach Matiang'i.

"We want to tell Matiangí that we shall impeach him in the next two weeks," said Sudi.
