Machakos: Woman Laid to Rest after Lying in Mortuary for 17 Years

- Esther Nzakwa Kitovo died on August 31, 2004, and her remains have been lying at the Machakos Funeral Home for 17 years

- A protracted land dispute delayed her burial as one of her step-sons filed a court case insisting he was the owner of the home where she was to be buried in 2004

- On Saturday, April 24, she was laid to rest in her son's home pending determination of the land case

A woman in Machakos who passed on in 2004 has been laid to rest 17 years after she breathed her last aged 93.

Esther Nzakwa Kitovo died on August 31, 2004, and her remains have been lying at the Machakos Funeral Home for 17 years due to a land dispute that halted her internment.

Reports indicate the woman was the first wife of her husband who died in 2000. He had three wives and according to the Kamba customs, the first wife is supposed to be buried alongside her husband.

However, plans to hold her funeral in 2004 flopped after Kitovu's step-son claimed to have an order from the Land Control Board that proved he was the owner of her matrimonial home.

Seventeen years later and many court cases, no judge had made the pronouncement that the 93-year-old was the rightful owner of the home.

On Saturday, April 24, she was laid to rest in her son's home pending determination of the land case.

Her funeral lasted a little over an hour and was held under the strict observance of COVID-19 rules.

"We feel relieved and we thank all who have supported us. We have cried and mourned," one of the woman's sons said.This is the end of a long and tormenting period. But we are happy that this has come to an end, and a peaceful end," another one said.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported about a Kirinyaga farmer who was laid to rest by his kinsmen in total silence in line with his wishes.

Samson Muriithi was buried inside the bedroom of his house in Mutaga village, Gichugu Constituency.

Before his death, the 60-year-old man had reportedly warned of dire consequences to anyone who would dare shed a tear during his burial.

The man reportedly instructed his family to bury him in his bedroom.
