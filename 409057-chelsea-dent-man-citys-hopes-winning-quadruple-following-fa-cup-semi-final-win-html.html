Chelsea End Man City's Hopes of Winning Quadruple after emphatic FA Cup Semi-Final Win

- Chelsea vs Man city ended 1-0 in favour of the London club in the first FA cup semi-finals

- Hakim Ziyechi scored what proved to be the match-winner in the second of the game

- Thomas Tuchel's men will now await the winner of the Southampton vs Leicester City game

Chelsea stormed into the finals of the FA Cup for the second season in a row following a narrow 1-0 win over Man City.

The only goal of the match was scored in the second half from Moroccan forward Hakim Ziyech.

The Blues were on the ascendancy from the first half and they looked at the more likely team to find the back of the net.

Chelsea thought they had taken the lead when Ziyechi tapped in a cross from Timo Werner but the referee's assistant raised the offside flag after the goal was scored.

It was Ben Chilwell's turn to come close to scoring as he volleyed into side netting from a Reece James' cross from the right.

Anther cross from Chilwell went agonizingly wide after taking a deflection off a City defender.

The Blues continued from where they stopped in the second half as Ziyech finally put them in the lead in the 55th minute.

Werner made a darting run down the left from a Mason Mount's through ball as he found Ziyech in the middle, and the Moroccan put the ball past the goalkeeper to make it 1-0.

Two minutes later it should have been 2-0, Ziyech capitalized on a poor clearance from City's defence but Zack Steffen came off his line at the right time to save the shot.

City had a shot on target from Rodri but the ball took a deflection to make the save easy for Kepa Arrizabalaga.

Christian Pulisic's goal in the 90th minute was also called off for offside after Kai Havertz's header played the American through on goal. The final whistle was blown as Chelsea made it through to the finals.

The Citizens hopes of winning a quadruple were dashed as Pep Guardiola and his men will need to focus on the Premier League, Champions League and the Carabao Cup.
