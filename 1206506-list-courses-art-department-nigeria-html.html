What are the courses in fine and applied arts?

Historically, painting, sculpturing, architecture, music, poetry, theatre, and dance are fine arts. Today, the list includes filmmaking, photography, design, and conceptual art. All the educational courses related to these studies are fine art courses.

Applied art, on the other hand, focuses on designing and decorating objects for everyday use. However, fine and applied art courses often overlap because you can use skills and knowledge gained in a fine art course to build an applied art career.

What are potential career paths for graduates of art?

Graduates of art department courses can pursue diverse career paths, such as becoming professional artists, art educators, graphic designers, art directors, museum curators, art therapists, fashion designers, illustrators, animators, or working in advertising and marketing agencies.

Why is an art course important?

Art courses give a forum for self-expression, allowing students to explore their artistic ability, experiment with various mediums, and establish their style. Furthermore, studying art helps improve critical thinking abilities, visual literacy, problem-solving skills, and the ability to think outside the box.
