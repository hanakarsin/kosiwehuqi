Ex-Mungiki leader Maina Njenga to vie for the Laikipia senatorial seat

- Former Mungiki leader Maina Njenga intends to vie for the Laikipia senatorial seat in 2017

- He says he will work with the Jubilee government adding that he is friends with President Uhuru Kenyatta and DP William Ruto

- Maina at one time led the dreaded Mungiki gang blamed for gorry killings 

Former Mungiki leader Maina Njenga has surfaced and declared that he will vie for a political seat in 2017.

Maina also revealed that he is a great friend to both president Uhuru Kenyatta and Deputy President William Ruto and intends to work with them.

According to media reports, Maina- who leads the Hope for International Ministries church- intends to vie for the Laikipia senatorial seat in the coming 2017 elections.

Maina, who led the notorious Mungiki gang several years ago and who claims to have reformed, said he is yet to decide which political vehicle he will use to vie for the seat.

The name of the former Mungiki leader turned preacher was mentioned during the trial of Uhuru and Ruto at the International Criminal Court in The Hague.

His now outlawed gang was accused of having carried out retaliatory attacks in Naivasha during the 2008 post-election violence.

These attacks were used as part of the evidence against President Uhuru but which ICC prosecutor Fatou Bensouda withdrew later.

Maina has survived several assassination attempts and was forced to move his church from Kitengela town following violence which led to the death of several of his congregants.

He intends to succeed GG Kariuki who is the current Laikipia senator.

Both Maina and Kariuki founded the Mkenya Solidarity Movement Party.
