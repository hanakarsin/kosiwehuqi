COVID-19: Donald Trump says he feels like kissing everyone after testing negative

- The White House said Monday Donald Trump had tested negative for COVID-19 consecutively after being treated and discharged

- Trump, while addressing resident of Florida, claimed he was immune to the disease and felt "powerful"

- On Monday, October 12, Twitter flagged Trump's message in which he claimed he was immune COVID-19 terming it as potentially harmful and misleading

The US President Donald Trump has said he "feels so good and powerful" after physicians at The White House confirmed he had tested negative for coronavirus.

Trump made the statements on Monday, October 12, in Florida where he addressed his first public rally after he was diagnosed with COVID-19 on Thursday, October 1.

In his speech, the US leader maintained his claims that he was not only immune to the new disease but could not also pass it on to other people adding that he felt like "kissing everyone in the audience".

"They now say that I am immune...some people have it is for life, others say it's for months while others argue that it is just for a few days...I don't know for how long. The cure (lockdown) cannot be worse then the problem itself.The good thing about this is that I went through it and now I feel so powerful...I feel like I will walk in that audience and kiss everyone. I will kiss the guys and the beautiful ladies. Everyone, I will just give you a big fat kiss," said Trump who entered the venue without a mask but dished out masks to his followers.

Tested negative

White House assistant press secretary Kayleigh McEnany said on Monday, October 12, said Trump had tested negative for COVID-19 consecutively using the Abbott BinaxNow antigen card.

"The comprehensive data is connected with the CDC's guidelines for removal of transmission-based precautions have informed our medical team's assessment that the president is not infectious to others, " said McEnany.

Trump's claims that he was immune to the new disease came hardly a day after Twitter fact-checked a message from the US head of state that claimed he was now resistant to the respiratory disease.

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), scientists are yet to establish whether or not contracting COVID-19 gives a person hard immunity which Trump claimed to have.

This could be the basis upon which Twitter used to flag the tweet and classify it as potentially misleading and harmful.

Earlier, Twitter added a warning notice on Trump's message which claimed COVID-19 was not as dangerous as the common cold.

He used his Monday speech to tear into Democratic presidential candidate Joe Biden whom he claimed will "reinstate lockdown and delay vaccine production" if elected US president.

Quick discharge

Trump also defended his quick discharge from the Walter Reed National Military Medical Center where he was admitted after his positive diagnosis stating that as a president, he could "not just lock yourself in a bunker" because of COVID-19.

"When you are the president, you cannot just lock yourself in a bunker and say that you are not going to bother with the world. I was not going to allow that anyway.You have to get out there and work...But I know it's risky. However, you get to have a good feeling when they say you are immune," Trump said amid cheers from supporters.
