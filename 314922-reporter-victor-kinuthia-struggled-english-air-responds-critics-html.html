Reporter Victor Kinuthia who struggled with English on air responds to critics

Inooro TV and radio presenter Victor Kinuthia has finally spoken after being turned into a social media’s punching bag for the better part of the night of Monday, September 9.

The presenter who fumbled with his words on live TV said he had given up all hope after discovering he turned into a laughing stock.

However, in a detailed Facebook post, Victor said his faith was rekindled when his bosses, friends and colleagues fiercely supported him.

According to the chap, he will rise again once the dust settles.

“I almost gave up when I found myself trending all over after yesterday's 9 pm news. But after receiving calls and texts from my bosses, colleagues, friends and fans I must say am encouraged,” he wrote.

This came shortly after TV bigwigs flooded Twitter with supportive messages cautioning the public against castigating a man who made a simple mistake.

Personalities like Victoria Rubadiri, Yvonne Okwara, Hussein Mohammed and Joe Ageyo among many others defended Victor’s honour.

To them, standing in front of a camera and fluently speaking without stuttering is never an easy task.

Each of them asked tweeps to cut the poor guy some slack and avoid poking fun at the disgraced presenter.

Victor attracted attention when he covered the arrest of Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro that took place on September, 10 2019 but fumbled while at it, with his not so good English becoming a point of focus.
