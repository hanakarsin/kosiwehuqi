Kenyan Woman Who Went to Saudi Arabia for Greener Pasture is Coming Back in a Coffin

  • Esther Wanjiku left the country in 2019 to look for greener pastures but report reached the family in May that she passed on in February
  • Her husband said the two had disagreed on the Saudi Arabia issue when she ran away from her matrimonial home and went back to her parent's place
  • The sister-in-law who was close to Esther said the lady suffered in the hands of a family that had both the son and his father interested in her

A Kenyan woman who left for Saudi Arabia for greener pastures has reportedly died.

According to reports reaching the family, Esther Wanjiku died in February 2021, but the family was alerted in May.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke's Lynn Ngugi, Esther's husband, Bernard Njenga, gave an account of what really transpired before and after she left the country.

Esther's tribulations

The mother of four told her husband in January 2019 that she wanted to go to Saudi Arabia but he was reluctant about it.

"When I told her that I do not like that idea, we started having small issues and shortly she ran away and went back to her parent's home. She carried the kids and went with them. I did not follow up with her immediately,'' Njenga narrated.

According to him, Esther had initially tried a hotel business and selling tea leaves but she was very ambitious and wanted to give her children a better life, which was the reason she wanted to go to Saudi Arabia.

"When she left for Saudi Arabia, she was in her mother's hands. I was never told, I actually learnt that she left from my sister, when she was at the airport and wanted to talk to our son. Only her mother can explain how she left, who facilitated her flight,'' Bernard said.

Her sister-in-law's account

The husband's sister Milka narrated that the two used to talk even when she travelled to Saudi Arabia and at one point she promised to pay her flight back to Kenya but she refused.

"On February 28, I messaged her because she had stayed for weeks without talking to anyone. So on March 5, I called her boss and asked him where Esther is, but he said Esther was no longer working there. I called the agent, and he never picked, so I wrote him a message."

A week later, the agent sent her a voice note saying that Esther had returned to Kenya. He went back to the boss and asked him to help her find Esther, but he later blocked her.

Later, she posted on Facebook and asked people for help and the next day she received a call from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
