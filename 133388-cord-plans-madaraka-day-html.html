CORD to hold parallel rally at Uhuru Park on Madaraka Day

- The CORD has indicated it will hold a parallel rally on Madaraka Day at Uhuru Park by writing to police demanding security come June 1, 2016

- The same day, President Uhuru Kenyatta will lead Madaraka Day celebrations in Nakuru's Afraha stadium

- CORD says the rally shall be used to celebrate liberties and freedoms and reaffirm the quest for constitutionalism in Kenya

- However, the removal of IEBC commissioners is expected to feature in the rally

The Coalition for Reforms and Democracy (CORD) is planning to hold a parallel rally come June 1, 2016, at Uhuru Park as President Uhuru Kenyatta leads Madaraka Day celebrations in Afraha stadium, Nakuru.

The CORD Secretariat, Norman Magaya, has written to Nairobi police boss Japheth Koome saying they have secured the venue for the rally and asking for security from the police to the leaders and their followers who are expected to attend.

According to the opposition, the rally is aimed at celebrating liberties and freedoms and reaffirm the quest for constitutionalism in Kenya.

However, the issue of the disbandment of the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) is not something the leaders are expected to skip.

The opposition has been holding anti-IEBC protests for four Mondays, demanding that the electoral bodyâ€™s commissioners vacate office over what they believe is gross misconduct.

The protests have seen police engage with demonstrators, lobbing teargas and using water canons to disperse them.

On Monday, May 23, at least four people were killed in Siaya and Kisumu counties when police alleged fired live bullets at demonstrators.

According to the government, a number of security officers were also injured in the melee. The officers were flown to Nairobi on Tuesday, May 24, for specialized treatment.

The CORD has temporarily suspended the planned demonstrations in what it says is giving room for dialogue from the Jubilee administration and allowing for peace.

The coalition has given Jubilee 10 days to institute dialogue failure to which it will continue with its demonstrations.

On its part, Jubilee has maintained that the opposition should follow the constitution if they want the commissioners out of office.

The government believes CORD has plans to overthrow it, claims the opposition has strongly denied.

IEBC chariman Issack Hassan has declared the commissioners would rather go to jail than resign. He says the commissioners have done no wrong to guarantee their removal from office.

It can be remembered IEBC was accused of receiving bribes from British firm Smith and Ouzman to give it tenders to supply election materials.

Two directors of the firm have since been jailed as Kenya drags itself to investigate the matter.
